8 Warning Signs of Electrical Problems — Ranked by How Fast to Act

Some of these can wait for a scheduled visit. Two of them mean leave the house. Here's the honest ranking.

Request a Quote Call (872) 378-0533

Published July 6, 2026 · 5 min read · MCL Electric

🚨 Act now — call any hour

1. Burning smell you can't locate — hot plastic or "fishy" odor near outlets, switches, or the panel. That's insulation cooking. Kill the circuit if you can identify it, and call: (872) 378-0533, 24/7. If the smell is strong and spreading, get out and call 911 first.

2. Scorch marks, sparking, or a hot panel — visible browning around an outlet, sparks bigger than a tiny static blip when plugging in, or a breaker panel that's warm/buzzing. Same category: emergency.

⚠️ Call this week

3. Breakers that trip repeatedly — one trip is protection; a pattern is a message. Our breaker guide explains how to read it.

4. Flickering or dimming when appliances kick on — lights dimming when the AC or dryer starts usually means loose connections or an overloaded service. Loose neutrals in particular get worse, not better.

5. Outlets or switches that are warm, loose, or crackle — heat at a device means resistance at a connection. This is the most common precursor we find behind melted devices.

6. Two-prong outlets or no GFCIs near water — kitchens, baths, laundry, and outdoor outlets need GFCI protection. Cheap to fix, and it's the code item inspectors flag most at sale.

📋 Schedule when convenient

7. A fuse box or a Federal Pacific / Zinsco panel — not an active fire today, but a known-hazard panel plus aging wiring is why insurers ask. Costs and timing in the panel guide.

8. Not enough outlets (extension cords everywhere) — permanent extension-cord living is how overload problems start. Adding circuits and outlets is routine work with an upfront price.

The 60-second rule of thumb

Heat, smell, or sound = now. Patterns = this week. Age and convenience = schedule it.
